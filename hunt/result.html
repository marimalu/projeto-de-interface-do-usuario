The pomegranate is the fruit of the pomegranate tree, common in the eastern Mediterranean and middle east where the pulp is used for the preparation of appetizers, dessert or sometimes in alcoholic beverage. Its interior is subdivided by thin films, which form small seeds possessing an edible pulp.
